_Formatting

- Bold: This text is bold

W3 notes, "The b element represents a span of text offset from its surrounding content without conveying any extra emphasis or importance, and for which the conventional typographic presentation is bold text; for example, keywords in a document abstract, or product names in a review."

On HTML5, W3 notes that "the element has now been given the specific semantic purpose of representing text 'offset from its surrounding content without conveying any extra emphasis or importance, and for which the conventional typographic presentation is bold text'”

- Strong: This text is strong

W3 notes, "The strong element represents a span of text with strong importance."

- Italic: This text is italic

W3 says, "The i element represents a span of text offset from its surrounding content without conveying any extra emphasis or importance, and for which the conventional typographic presentation is italic text; for example, a taxonomic designation, a technical term, an idiomatic phrase from another language, a thought, or a ship name."

On HTML5, W3 notes, "the element has now been given the specific semantic purpose of representing text 'offset from its surrounding content without conveying any extra emphasis or importance, and for which the conventional typographic presentation is italic text'”

- Emphasized: This text is emphasized

On HTML5, W3 notes, "The em element represents a span of text with emphatic stress."

Note: W3Schools notes, "Browsers display < strong > as < b >, and < em > as < i >. However, there is a difference in the meaning of these tags: < b > and < i > defines bold and italic text, but < strong > and < em > means that the text is 'important'".

- Small: The text here is small, isn't it?

- Mark: The text here is marked or "highlighted"

- Del: The text here is given the appearance of being deleted, or removed.

- Ins: The text here is given the appearance of being inserted, or added.
